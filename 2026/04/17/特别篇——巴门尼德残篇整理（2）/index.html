特别篇——巴门尼德残篇整理（2）

发表于2026-04-17|更新于2026-04-18|西方哲学
|浏览量:

封面来自安德烈.塔可夫斯基《乡愁》

巴门尼德残篇整理共有三篇，第三篇为意见之路内容，暂时不更新。英文内容来自Patricia Curd编著的A Presocratics Reader，直译自古希腊文，因此可能不符合现代英语语法。汉语内容以《西方哲学原著选读》为基础，进行了适当改编，虽然仍然可能不那么准确（毕竟这需要回溯性建构）对内容的具体理解见“读《巴门尼德的遗产》”这一专题，此专题仅呈现内容的形式。文中数字代表汇编原文的具体行数，由于排版问题无法呈现原貌。

（B4）But gaze upon things which although absent are securely present to the mind.For you will not cut off what-is from clinging to what-is, neither being scattered everywhere in every way in order nor being brought together.

但你要凝视那些虽不在场，却对心灵可靠地在场的东西。因为你不能把“是者”从与“是者”的维系中切断，既不能让它到处，以一切方式，有秩序地被分散，也不能让它被聚合。

（B5）For me, it is indifferent from where I am to begin: for that is where I will arrive back again.

对我来说，无论从哪里开始，都没有区别：因为我总会回到那里。

（B6）It is right both to say and to think that it is what-is: for it can be,but nothing is not: these things I bid you to ponder.For I <？> you from this first route of inquiry,and then from that, on which mortals, knowing nothing,wander, two-headed: for helplessness in their breasts steers their wandering mind. They are borne along deaf and blind alike ,dazed, hordes without judgment for whom to be and not to be are thought to be the same and not the same, and the path of all is backward-turning.

必须这样说，也必须这样想：是者是，因为它“是”是可能的，而无（不是者）不是：这些事我敦促你深思。这就是我吩咐你注意的第一条研究途径。然后你还要注意另一条途径；在那条途径上，那些什么都不明白的凡人们两头彷徨。因为他们的心中不知所措，被摇摆不定的念头支配着，所以像聋子和瞎子一样无所适从。这些不能分辨是非的群氓，居然认为是和不是同一又不同一，而一切的道路都是折返之途。

（B7）For in no way may this prevail, that things that are not are; but you, hold your thought back from this route of inquiry and do not let habit, rich in experience, compel you along this route to direct an aimless eye and an echoing ear and tongue, but judge by reasoning (logos) the much-contested 5 examination spoken by me.

因为‘是者不是’这一点，绝不可能得逞。但你要把你的思想从这条探究之路上拉回来，也不要让那经验丰富的习惯强迫你沿着这条路，去动用那无目的的眼睛，鸣响的耳朵和舌头，而要用理性（逻各斯）来判断我所提出的这场备受争议的检验。

由于（B8）过长，因此分段进行，大约每十行分成一段。

（B8.1-10）Just one story of a route is still left: that it is. On this route there are signs very many, that what-is is ungenerated and imperishable, a whole of a single kind, unshaken, and complete.Nor was it ever, nor will it be, since it is now, all together 5 one, holding together: For what birth will you seek out for it? How and from what did it grow? From what-is-not I will allow you neither to say nor to think: For it is not to be said or thought that it is not. What need would have roused it, later or earlier, having begun from nothing, to grow?

仅有一条路还留着：即“它是”。在这条路上有许多标志：是者非被生成且不灭，是单一种类的整体，不动摇，且彻底。它既不曾是，也不将是，因为它现在是，全都在一处，是一，紧聚着：你将给它寻出什么来源呢？它如何、从何生长？从不是者中，我既不允许你说，也不允许你想：因为“它不是”既不可说也不可想。什么需要会催逼它，更迟或更早，从无开始，生长出来？

（B8.11-20）In this way it is right either fully to be or not.Nor will the force of true conviction ever permit anything to come to be beside it from what-is-not. For this reason neither coming to be nor perishing did Justice allow, loosening her shackles,but she （Justice）holds it fast. And the decision about these things is in this: 15 is or is not; and it has been decided, as is necessary,to leave the one route thought of and unnamed (for it is not a true route), so that the other route is and is genuine.But how can what-is be hereafter? How can it come to be?For if it came to be, it is not, not even if it is sometime going to be

如此，它必须要么完全地是，要么不是。确信之力也永不允许任何东西从不是者中，在它之外生成。因此，正义女神既不放松镣铐，允许它生成，也不允许它毁灭，而是她牢牢握住。关于这些事的裁决在于此：是或不是；而它已被裁决，如必然所是的那样，将一条路留作不被思且不被命名（因为它不是一条真的路），以便另一条路是且是真实的。但是者如何能在以后是？它如何能生成？因为若它曾生成，它便不是，哪怕它某时将要是。

（B8.21-31）Thus coming-to-be has been extinguished and perishing cannot be investigated. Nor is it divisible, since it is all alike,and not at all more in any way, which would keep it from holding together,or at all less, but it is all full of what-is.Therefore it is all holding together; for what-is draws near to what-is. 25 But unchanging in the limits of great bonds it is without starting or ceasing, since coming-to-be and perishing have wandered very far away; and true trust drove them away.Remaining the same and in the same and by itself it lies and so remains there fixed; for mighty Necessity 30 holds it in bonds of a limit which holds it in on all sides.

据此，生成已被熄灭，且毁灭无从察究。它亦不可分，因它全然相同，既不以任何方式有丝毫更多，那会阻碍它紧聚相连，亦无丝毫更少，而是它全然充盈着是者。因此，它全然紧聚相连；因是者趋近于是者。然于巨链之界限内，它无有变动，既无开端亦无终止，因生成与毁灭已被驱至极远之处；真实的信赖将其逐离。保持同一、在同一中，且凭靠自身，它保持且坚固保持；因强大的必然（女神）以界限之链从四面围束，将其握持。

（B8.32-41）For this reason it is right for what-is to be not incomplete;for it is not lacking; otherwise, what-is would be in want of everything.What is for thinking is the same as that on account of which there is thought.For not without what-is, on which it depends, having been solemnly pronounced,will you find thinking; for nothing else either is or will be except what-is, since precisely this is what Fate shackled to be whole and changeless. Therefore it has been named all things that mortals, persuaded that they are true, have posited both to come to be and to perish, to be and not,and to change place and alter bright color.

因此之故，是者不是不完整的，是必然的；因为它不欠缺；否则，是者会匮乏一切。为思而是的东西，与思想因之而有的那个东西，是同一个。因为没有是者——思于其上被庄严宣告而依存——你将找不到思；因为既无其他东西是，也将无其他东西是，除了是者之外，既然正是这个被命运捆缚为完整且不变。因此，一切东西已被命名，这些东西，有死者们，被说服认为它们是真的，设定为既生成又毁灭，既是又不是，且改变处所并变换明亮的颜色

（B8.42-52）But since the limit is ultimate, it （namely, what-is） is complete from all directions like the bulk of a ball well-rounded from all sides equally matched in every way from the middle; for it is right for it to be not in any way greater or lesser than in another. For neither is there what-is-not—which would stop it from reaching the same—nor is there any way in which what-is would be more than what-is in one way and in another way less, since it is all inviolable; for equal to itself from all directions, it meets uniformly with its limits. At this point, I end for you my reliable account and thought about truth. From here on, learn mortal opinions, listening to the deceitful order of my words.

但既然界限是终极的，它（即，存在者）是完满的，从所有方向，像一个球体，从所有面被良好地弄圆，从中心出发在每一方面同等地匹配；因为它不在任何方面比在另一方面更大或更小，这是正确的。因为既没有不是者——它会阻止是者抵达相同——也没有任何方式，在其中是者会在一种方式上比是者更多，而在另一种方式上更少，因为它是全然不可侵犯的；因为从所有方向与自身相等，它均匀地遇到它的各个界限。至此，我为你结束我关于真理的可靠叙述与思。从此处起，学习凡人的意见，倾听我话语的欺人秩序。
