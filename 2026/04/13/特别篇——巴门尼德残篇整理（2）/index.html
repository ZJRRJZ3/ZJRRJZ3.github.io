特别篇——巴门尼德残篇整理（2）

发表于2026-04-13|更新于2026-04-17|西方哲学
|浏览量:

封面来自黑泽明《梦》

英文内容来自Patricia Curd整理的A Presocratic Reader，中文翻译是基于英文原文并结合《西方哲学原著选读》进行的改编，毕竟《选读》一书进行了一部分“意译”内容。残篇整理共有三篇内容，不对残篇进行解读，只呈现内容。英文内容来自于对古希腊原文的直译，因此可能不符合现代英语语法，段落中出现的数字代表原文对应行数，由于排版原因无法做到对原文形式的呈现。

（B4）But gaze upon things which although absent are securely present to the mind.For you will not cut off what-is from clinging to what-is, neither being scattered everywhere in every way in order nor being brought together.

但你要凝视那些虽不在场，却对心灵可靠地在场的东西。因为你不能把“是者”从与“是者”的维系中切断，既不能让它到处，以一切方式，有秩序地被分散，也不能让它被聚合。

（B5）For me, it is indifferent from where I am to begin: for that is where I will arrive back again.

对我来说，无论从哪里开始，都没有区别：因为我总会回到那里。

（B6）It is right both to say and to think that it is what-is: for it can be, but nothing is not: these things I bid you to ponder. For I <？> you from this first route of inquiry, and then from that, on which mortals, knowing nothing, wander, two-headed: for helplessness in their breasts steers their wandering mind. They are borne along deaf and blind alike, dazed, hordes without judgment for whom to be and not to be are thought to be the same and not the same, and the path of all is backward-turning.

问号处为原文本缺失

必须这样说，也必须这样想：是者是，因为它是是可能的，但无则不是。

（B7）For in no way may this prevail, that things that are not are; but you, hold your thought back from this route of inquiry and do not let habit, rich in experience, compel you along this route to direct an aimless eye and an echoing ear and tongue, but judge by reasoning (logos) the much-contested 5 examination spoken by me.

因为‘是者不是’这一点，绝不可能得逞。但你要把你的思想从这条探究之路上拉回来，也不要让那经验丰富的习惯强迫你沿着这条路，去动用那无目的的眼睛，鸣响的耳朵和舌头，而要用理性（逻各斯）来判断我所提出的这场备受争议的检验。

（B10）You shall know the nature of the Aithēr and all the signs in the Aithēr and the destructive works of the shining sun’s pure torch and whence they came to be, and you shall learn the wandering works of the round-faced moon and its nature, and you shall know also the surrounding heaven, 5 from what it grew and how Necessity led and shackled it to hold the limits of the stars.

你将知晓以太的本性，以及以太中所有的征兆，还有那灼灼烈日纯净火炬的毁灭功绩以及它们从何而生，你还将了解圆面月亮的漫游事迹及其本性，你还将认识那环绕的苍穹，它从何生长而出，必然性又如何牵引并束缚着它，去执掌群星的限制。
